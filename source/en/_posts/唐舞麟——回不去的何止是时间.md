# <span class="c-red">Seed of the Golden Dragon King — Tang Wulin</span>

{% alertBlockquote info %}
This covers the third part of the "Soul Land" series — *The Legend of the Dragon King*, my personal favorite.
{% endalertBlockquote %}

## His Origins

Tang Wulin was born with the <span class="c-red">Golden Dragon King</span> bloodline, sealed within him by his father Tang San. Placed in a humble family in Glorybound City, he grew up far from the Divine Realm, unaware of his true heritage.

At age six, his martial soul awakened: <span class="c-blue">Bluesilver Grass</span> — the weakest martial soul there is. The village children laughed at him. But his father had long ago proven that Bluesilver Grass could become a god — and Tang Wulin's version was fused with the Golden Dragon King's essence, making it far more than it appeared.

## The Dragon King's Path

From Glorybound City to Shrek Academy, from a boy mocked for his "useless" martial soul to the leader of his generation — every breakthrough came at the cost of blood and tears.

{% alertBlockquote info %}
What makes Tang Wulin special isn't his bloodline — it's his heart. He carries the weight of an entire era on his shoulders, yet never loses the warmth that makes him human.
{% endalertBlockquote %}

## The People Around Him

{% alertBlockquote danger %}
**Gu Yuena** — The Silver Dragon King, his destined love and his destined enemy. Two souls bound across reincarnations, yet fated to stand on opposite sides. Their story is the emotional core of the entire series.
{% endalertBlockquote %}

{% alertBlockquote info %}
**Wu Zhangkong** — Shrek Academy's outer court dean, Tang Wulin's teacher. White robes, blue sword, ice and snow. He dedicated his life to Shrek, but his heart belonged to the lover frozen in his arms.

His Skyfrost martial soul and his love for Long Bing make him one of the most tragic figures in the series. When Tang Wulin inscribed "Disciple, Tang Wulin" at his grave, every stroke of the Golden Dragon Spear carried his grief.
{% endalertBlockquote %}

To Tang Wulin, Wu Zhangkong was more than a teacher — he was a <span class="c-blue">mentor</span>, an <span class="c-purple">older brother</span>, even a <span class="c-red">father figure</span>.

## His Companions

Along the way, he gathered a team of loyal friends — each with their own dreams and struggles. Together they faced trials that would break lesser students, forging bonds stronger than any soul ring.

## Final Words

Tang Wulin is not a perfect protagonist. He makes mistakes, carries regrets, and bears burdens no one should have to carry. But that's exactly why he feels real — more real than any invincible hero ever could.

{% alertBlockquote tip %}
